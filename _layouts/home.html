I study phonology, the linguistic knowledge of sound patterns, and its interaction with other modules such as morphology. The research goal of phonology and its interfaces is to understand the mental representations and computations, as well as the grammatical architecture in the human mind. In particular, I investigated the phenomena at the phonology-morphology interface and the phonology-phonetics interface in various languages, with my fieldwork data or existing corpora.

My recent research projects feature Huozhou Chinese and Ladin. For Huozhou, I focus on the patterns of diminutive formation and explore how understudied Chinese dialects can inform phonological representation and grammar. For Ladin, my current work involves description and analyses of phonotactics with fieldwork data and acoustic experiments.
