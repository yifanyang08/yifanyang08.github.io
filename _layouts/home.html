I am a recent graduate from the Department of Linguistics, University of Southern California. I study phonology, the linguistic knowledge of sound patterns, and its interaction with other modules such as morphology. The research goal of phonology and its interfaces is to understand the mental representations and computations, and the grammatical architecture in the human mind.

My recent research projects feature Huozhou Chinese and Ladin. The data of both languages are mainly based on my fieldwork. For Huozhou, I focus on the patterns of diminutive formation and explore how understudied Chinese dialects can inform phonological representation and grammar. For Ladin, my current work involves description and analyses of its phonotactics. An ongoing project is to investigate the temporal coordination of onset clusters in a variety of Ladin. More details about my research can be found here.
